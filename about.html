About Spring Lake Farms

Our History

Founded in 1920 by Bill Hardy, our farm has grown from less than 10 cows to nearly 500 today. Now spanning an impressive 800 acres, Spring Lake Farms is proudly managed by the fourth generation of the Hardy family, Bill and Jason Hardy. With a commitment to quality and sustainability, we milk 180 cows twice a day, producing an astounding 2,000 gallons of milk daily. Our milk is carefully collected and sold to local dairies, where it is transformed into delicious cheeses that our community enjoys.

Throughout the years, we have embraced modern farming practices while honoring our family traditions, ensuring that our farm not only thrives but also contributes positively to the environment and our local economy. As we continue to grow, we remain dedicated to nurturing our land and livestock, fostering strong relationships within our community, and providing the freshest, highest-quality dairy products possible.

Meet the Owners

Bill Hardy

Bill Hardy graduated from Michigan State University and plays a key role in managing the cows at Spring Lake Farms, focusing on milking and breeding. Bill's love for the outdoors is reflected in his hobbies, which include nature exploration, hunting trips, and fishing. He’s also a big fan of baseball and softball, both as a player and spectator. Bill is married to his wife, Jen, and they have two children.

Jason Hardy

Jason Hardy graduated from Michigan State University with a degree in Animal Science. He plays a key role in maintaining the farm, spending much of his time during the summer working in the fields and operating machinery. A true outdoorsman, Jason enjoys being close to nature and spends his free time hunting. He’s also a big fan of homemade apple pie. Jason is happily married to his wife, Jeri, and together they have five children, continuing the proud Hardy family tradition at Spring Lake Farms.

A Day in the Life at Spring Lake Farms

Each day at Spring Lake Farms begins before dawn, as we rise at 3 a.m. to start the first milking of our cows. The tranquility of the early morning hours sets the tone for our day as we ensure each cow is milked with care and precision. After the morning milking, we feed our cows, providing them with the nutrients they need to stay healthy and productive. Throughout the day, we attend to various tasks, from fixing broken equipment to maintaining our barns and fields. In the afternoon, we prepare for the second milking session at 3 p.m., where we repeat the routine with dedication. Our commitment to our animals and our farm is unwavering, and each day is a blend of hard work, passion, and a deep respect for the land that sustains us.
